How I got here

You do not care why I want to write. You do not care if I write. But since this is for me, I will just talk about what I want.

Bloging with the tools I know

I wanted to automate this for a couple of reasons:

  • If it isn't simple, I won't do it
  • If I can't automate it, I won't do it
  • If I don't understand it I can't automate it and I won't do it

My requirements

I need any solution I write in to be:

  • Emacs based
  • Remain close to bare bones
  • Be simple
  • Be scriptable

Really point one solves all the other problems. Yes it could have gotten complicated, but I fought to keep complications at bay.

I tried (hugo, jekyll, and one.el) but they all failed for one of the reasons listed above. Fortunately GNU Emacs already had everything I needed to do this built in. It takes a bit of setup and testing. A lot of thought on the work flow. And in a sense I am still working out the kinks.

  • [X] setup Org Mode for publishing
  • [X] get the directory structure right
  • [X] develop some assets (images, css, etc.)
  • [X] incorporate denote in the workflow

I am still testing the denote integration. It involved saving all my original notes which were cluttered and not useable for something that I want to be more structured and clean. I will eventually bring some of my original notes back into the fold, but for now they are banished.

a little update <2024-01-30 Tue>

I am gonna have to say I have been using this (GNU Emacs mostly + denote.el) workflow for a day now and I really am glad I didn't go with another solution. Everything just works. Inserting links and code examples are all second nature because its how I have been working all along. The one thing that I have noticed is that putting a bit more thought into what I am capturing, why I am capturing it, and where it is stored has been freeing.
